Penguin Safety and Handling

Introduction to penguin safety

Penguins are fascinating and friendly animals, but it's important to approach them with care and respect. As part of The Penguin Company, our top priority is ensuring the well-being of our rescued penguins and providing a safe environment for guests. Penguins can be sensitive to human interaction, and their unique needs must be considered during every encounter. Following safety guidelines will ensure that both you and the penguins have a fun, stress-free experience.

Penguin handling dos and don'ts

Dos

  • Follow the handler's instructions at all times to ensure the penguin's comfort and safety.
  • Approach penguins slowly and calmly. Sudden movements may startle them.
  • Maintain a respectful distance until the penguin approaches or until directed by a handler.
  • Watch for signs of stress, such as rapid head movements or vocalizations. Inform your handler if you notice them.
  • Wash your hands before and after interacting with the penguins to avoid spreading germs.

Don'ts

  • Don't touch the penguin's back or face. They may feel threatened by this.
  • Don't make loud noises or sudden actions that could scare the penguin.
  • Don't chase or try to pick up a penguin without the handler's permission.
  • Don't feed the penguins unless instructed to by the handler. They have specific diets and other foods may hurt them.
  • Don't crowd the penguins. Give them space to move freely and retreat if they feel uncomfortable.
